总叙 · Preface

弟子规　圣人训
Standards for students — teachings of the sages.
首孝弟　次谨信
First, be filial and fraternal; next, be careful and trustworthy.
泛爱众　而亲仁
Love all broadly and draw close to the benevolent.
有余力　则学文
With strength remaining, study the classics and literature.

入则孝 · Filial Piety at Home

父母呼　应勿缓
When parents call, answer without delay.
父母命　行勿懒
When parents instruct, act without laziness.
父母教　须敬听
When they teach, listen respectfully.
父母责　须顺承
When they correct, accept with compliance.
冬则温　夏则凊
Keep them warm in winter and cool in summer.
晨则省　昏则定
Attend to them in the morning and settle them at night.
出必告　反必面
Inform them when you go out; greet them face-to-face when you return.
居有常　业无变
Keep regular habits; do not change your proper work.
事虽小　勿擅为
Though a matter is small, do not act on your own.
苟擅为　子道亏
If you do so, you fail in the way of a child.
物虽小　勿私藏
Though an item is small, do not keep it secretly.
苟私藏　亲心伤
If you do, you hurt your parents’ hearts.
亲所好　力为具
Provide diligently what your parents like.
亲所恶　谨为去
Remove carefully what your parents dislike.
身有伤　贻亲忧
If your body is harmed, you cause your parents worry.
德有伤　贻亲羞
If your virtue is harmed, you bring your parents shame.
亲爱我　孝何难
If parents love me, filial piety is easy.
亲憎我　孝方贤
If parents dislike me, being filial is truly virtuous.
亲有过　谏使更
If parents err, remonstrate to help them change.
怡吾色　柔吾声
Do so with a pleasant expression and gentle voice.
谏不入　悦复谏
If counsel is not accepted, wait and advise again.
号泣随　挞无怨
Even with tears and beating, bear no resentment.
亲有疾　药先尝
If parents are ill, taste the medicine first.
昼夜侍　不离床
Attend by day and night, not leaving the bedside.
丧三年　常悲咽
During three years’ mourning, remain sorrowful.
居处变　酒肉绝
Change your dwelling and abstain from wine and meat.
丧尽礼　祭尽诚
Fulfill mourning rites and sacrifice with sincerity.
事死者　如事生
Serve the departed as if they were alive.

出则弟 · Fraternal Duty & Respect Outside

兄道友　弟道恭
Elder brothers should be friendly; younger brothers respectful.
兄弟睦　孝在中
Harmony among siblings is itself filial piety.
财物轻　怨何生
Hold property lightly; how can resentment arise?
言语忍　忿自泯
Endure in speech; anger will fade.
或饮食　或坐走
Whether eating, sitting, or walking,
长者先　幼者后
Let elders go first; the young go after.
长呼人　即代叫
If elders call someone, call on their behalf.
人不在　己即到
If the person is absent, go yourself.
称尊长　勿呼名
When addressing elders, do not call by given name.
对尊长　勿见能
Before elders, do not show off your ability.
路遇长　疾趋揖
On meeting elders on the road, hasten and bow.
长无言　退恭立
If they do not speak, withdraw and stand respectfully.
骑下马　乘下车
Dismount from horse or carriage before elders.
过犹待　百步余
When passing by, wait even a hundred steps away.
长者立　幼勿坐
If elders stand, the young should not sit.
长者坐　命乃坐
Sit only when elders invite you to sit.
尊长前　声要低
Before elders, keep your voice low.
低不闻　却非宜
Yet too low to hear is also improper.
进必趋　退必迟
When approaching, quicken your step; when leaving, do so slowly.
问起对　视勿移
When asked, rise to answer; do not avert your gaze.
事诸父　如事父
Serve paternal uncles as father.
事诸兄　如事兄
Serve elder cousins/brothers as elder brothers.

谨 · Cautious Conduct

朝起早　夜眠迟
Rise early; retire with purpose.
老易至　惜此时
Old age comes easily; cherish your time.
晨必盥　兼漱口
Wash in the morning and rinse your mouth.
便溺回　辄净手
After using the toilet, always wash hands.
冠必正　纽必结
Cap straight, buttons fastened.
袜与履　俱紧切
Socks and shoes fitted and secure.
置冠服　有定位
Place clothes and cap in fixed positions.
勿乱顿　致污秽
Do not toss them about and make them dirty.
衣贵洁　不贵华
Value cleanliness over extravagance.
上循分　下称家
Act within your means, fitting your family.
对饮食　勿拣择
At meals be undemanding; do not be picky.
食适可　勿过则
Eat to sufficiency; avoid excess.
年方少　勿饮酒
In youth, do not drink alcohol.
饮酒醉　最为丑
Drunkenness is most disgraceful.
步从容　立端正
Walk with composure; stand upright.
揖深圆　拜恭敬
Bows should be deep and rounded; salute with respect.
勿践阈　勿跛倚
Do not tread on thresholds; do not lean or hobble.
勿箕踞　勿摇髀
Do not sprawl with legs spread; do not shake your thighs.
缓揭帘　勿有声
Lift curtains slowly and silently.
宽转弯　勿触棱
Turn corners with space; avoid hitting edges.
执虚器　如执盈
Hold empty vessels as if full.
入虚室　如有人
Enter empty rooms as if someone were there.
事勿忙　忙多错
Do not act in haste; haste breeds mistakes.
勿畏难　勿轻略
Do not fear difficulty; do not treat things lightly.
斗闹场　绝勿近
Avoid brawls and places of disorder.
邪僻事　绝勿问
Do not inquire into deviant matters.
将入门　问孰存
Before entering, ask who is present.
将上堂　声必扬
Before entering the hall, raise your voice to announce.
人问谁　对以名
If asked who you are, answer with your name.
吾与我　不分明
‘I’ and ‘me’—do not be unclear.
用人物　须明求
When using others’ things, clearly ask first.
倘不问　即为偷
If you do not ask, it is stealing.
借人物　及时还
Return borrowed items promptly.
后有急　借不难
Then when urgent later, borrowing is not hard.

信 · Trustworthiness

凡出言　信为先
In all speech, let trust come first.
诈与妄　奚可焉
Deceit and falsehood—how can they be?
话说多　不如少
Better few words than many.
惟其是　勿佞巧
Speak only what is right; avoid flattery or craftiness.
奸巧语　秽污词
Cunning words and foul language—
市井气　切戒之
Vulgar street tone—strictly avoid.
见未真　勿轻言
What you have not seen true—do not speak lightly.
知未的　勿轻传
What you do not know for sure—do not spread lightly.
事非宜　勿轻诺
If a matter is improper, do not promise lightly.
苟轻诺　进退错
If you do, you will err in advance and retreat.
凡道字　重且舒
When speaking, be weighty and measured.
勿急疾　勿模糊
Do not be hasty or vague.
彼说长　此说短
Praising one, belittling another—
不关己　莫闲管
If it does not concern you, do not meddle.
见人善　即思齐
Seeing others’ goodness, think to equal it.
纵去远　以渐跻
Even if far from it, approach gradually.
见人恶　即内省
Seeing others’ faults, examine yourself.
有则改　无加警
If you have them, correct; if not, be more alert.
唯德学　唯才艺
In virtue, learning, talent, and skill—
不如人　当自砺
If inferior to others, you must sharpen yourself.
若衣服　若饮食
As for clothing and food—
不如人　勿生戚
If not as good as others, do not be sad.
闻过怒　闻誉乐
Angry at criticism, pleased by praise—
损友来　益友却
Such harms attract bad friends and repel good ones.
闻誉恐　闻过欣
Be wary of praise and glad to hear faults.
直谅士　渐相亲
Then upright, honest people will draw near.
无心非　名为错
Faults without intent are called mistakes.
有心非　名为恶
Faults with intent are called evil.
过能改　归于无
If you can correct faults, they return to nothing.
倘掩饰　增一辜
If you cover them up, you add another guilt.

泛爱众 · Universal Love

凡是人　皆须爱
All people should be loved.
天同覆　地同载
Heaven covers us alike; Earth bears us alike.
行高者　名自高
Those of high conduct have high repute.
人所重　非貌高
What people esteem is not lofty looks.
才大者　望自大
Those of great talent have great expectations.
人所服　非言大
What people admire is not big talk.
己有能　勿自私
If you have ability, do not be selfish.
人所能　勿轻訾
Do not belittle what others can do.
勿谄富　勿骄贫
Do not fawn on the rich or be proud in poverty.
勿厌故　勿喜新
Do not despise the old or only like the new.
人不闲　勿事搅
If people are busy, do not disturb them.
人不安　勿话扰
If people are ill at ease, do not trouble them with talk.
人有短　切莫揭
Do not expose others’ shortcomings.
人有私　切莫说
Do not divulge others’ privacy.
道人善　即是善
Speaking of others’ good is itself good.
人知之　愈思勉
When others know it, they will be more encouraged.
扬人恶　即是恶
Proclaiming others’ evil is itself evil.
疾之甚　祸且作
If extreme in this, disaster will follow.
善相劝　德皆建
Urging each other to goodness builds virtue for both.
过不规　道两亏
Not correcting faults harms both parties.
凡取与　贵分晓
In taking and giving, clarity matters.
与宜多　取宜少
Give more; take less.
将加人　先问己
Before imposing on others, ask yourself first.
己不欲　即速已
If you would not want it, stop at once.
恩欲报　怨欲忘
Repay kindness; forget resentment.
报怨短　报恩长
Repaying resentment is brief; repaying kindness is long.
待婢仆　身贵端
Treat servants with dignity and uprightness.
虽贵端　慈而宽
Be kind and lenient even when dignified.
势服人　心不然
Power forces obedience, but not the heart.
理服人　方无言
Reason convinces and leaves no words unsaid.

亲仁 · Draw Near to the Virtuous

同是人　类不齐
Though all are people, dispositions are not the same.
流俗众　仁者希
Common customs are many; the benevolent are few.
果仁者　人多畏
Truly benevolent people are often revered.
言不讳　色不媚
They do not mince words nor flatter in looks.
能亲仁　无限好
Keeping close to the benevolent brings endless good.
德日进　过日少
Virtue increases daily; faults decrease daily.
不亲仁　无限害
Failing to be close to them brings endless harm.
小人进　百事坏
The petty advance, and a hundred things go wrong.

余力学文 · Study With Remaining Energy

不力行　但学文
If you do not practice but only study words—
长浮华　成何人
You become long on show and shallow—what kind of person is that?
但力行　不学文
If you only practice but do not study—
任己见　昧理真
You follow your own views and miss true principles.
读书法　有三到
The method of reading has three arrivals—
心眼口　信皆要
Heart, eyes, and mouth: all must be sincere.
方读此　勿慕彼
While reading this, do not yearn for that.
此未终　彼勿起
Before finishing this, do not start another.
宽为限　紧用功
Set broad limits; apply effort tightly.
工夫到　滞塞通
With sufficient effort, blockages will clear.
心有疑　随札记
If doubts arise, note them down.
就人问　求确义
Ask others to seek the exact meaning.
房室清　墙壁净
Keep rooms clean and walls tidy.
几案洁　笔砚正
Desks clean; brush and inkstone set straight.
墨磨偏　心不端
If grinding ink askew, the mind is not upright.
字不敬　心先病
If characters lack respect, the heart is already ill.
列典籍　有定处
Place books in fixed positions.
读看毕　还原处
After reading, return them to their places.
虽有急　卷束齐
Even in haste, roll and bind them neatly.
有缺坏　就补之
If damaged, repair them.
非圣书　屏勿视
Non-classic harmful books—avoid looking at them.
蔽聪明　坏心志
They obscure wisdom and harm the will.
勿自暴　勿自弃
Do not despise or abandon yourself.
圣与贤　可驯致
Sages and worthies can be gradually attained.
